Tessera

The trust engine for AI-generated compliance evidence. Four rival AI models debate real Splunk data, then seal a tamper-evident verdict you can verify.

Live: aec3.accessquint.com · github.com/sandhipveera/audit-evidence-compiler

The problem
  • A single SOC 2 audit cycle = 40+ hours of pulling evidence from Splunk and judging whether it satisfies the control.
  • AI can generate that evidence in seconds — but can you trust it? One model, one opinion, no proof it wasn't altered.
  • 90% of orgs fail their first AI-governance audit — they have policies, not proof.
The bottleneck isn't generating evidence. It's trusting it.
The idea

Don't trust one AI — make four debate

PersonaModelLens
AuditorClaude · Anthropicsatisfies the control language?
EngineerGPT-5.5 / Codex · OpenAIstatistically sound?

Consensus is mechanical — lowest verdict wins, no LLM tiebreaker → reproducible. One dissent forces PARTIAL/FAIL.

Deep Splunk integration

Real Splunk, end to end

  • Question → SPL generated → policy gate → executed on BOTS v3 (1.94M real events) via MCP.
  • The Adversary can issue counter-SPL — a 2nd round runs on what the data actually shows.
  • Verdict + Merkle root posted back to Splunk (HEC → index=aec_audit) — a live audit log inside the platform.
  • Splunk-native Compliance Posture dashboard + | auditcompiler search command.

Not mocked — live tunnel to the Splunk instance, real query results, real write-back.

Trust, made provable

Tamper-evident by construction

  • Every snapshot is SHA-256 Merkle-chained (canonical JSON; no signing keys to leak).
  • The gap_report.xlsx carries the chain root.
  • Anyone verifies at the public /verify portal — drop in audit_trail.jsonl:
  •   ✅ VERIFIED — nothing changed since collection  ·  ❌ TAMPERED — one flipped verdict breaks the hash.
Trust isn't asserted. It's provable — by a third party, with no install.
Use cases

Four ways teams use it

  • 40 hours → 30 seconds — SOC 2 CC6.1 audit prep, compiled in one run.
  • Passed in Q1, failing now — control drift across two Splunk windows.
  • Verify, then trust — four rival models cross-check every verdict; tampering is provable.
  • AI-governance audit (ISO 42001) — "policies, not proof" → provable AI-control evidence from Splunk.

Five frameworks: SOC 2 · ISO 27001:2022 · NIST CSF 2.0 · NIST 800-53 Rev 5 · COBIT 2019.
